## Report: Research Proposal & Summary of Thinking Process

**Executive Summary:** This report outlines the research proposal, including the proposed methodology for evaluating carbon footprint in cardboard packaging industry. 

**Research Problem:** The increased focus on sustainability has led to a rising demand for eco-friendly materials and production processes within the cardboard packaging sector.  Understanding and minimizing environmental impact is crucial, particularly regarding carbon emissions related to manufacturing and transportation. However, existing methods may not be comprehensive or precise enough for detailed analysis of the entire supply chain.

**Research Question:** How can material flow cost accounting (MFCA) effectively identify carbon footprint sources within the cardboard packaging industry?

**Methodology**: 

1. **Literature Review & Data Extraction:** The research team will conduct a thorough literature review to understand existing methodologies and frameworks for assessing carbon emissions in the paper and packaging sector. This includes exploring studies on MFCA, life-cycle assessment (LCA), material flow analysis (MFA) and their applications within specific industries. 
2. **Case Study Analysis:** A case study approach will be used to investigate a specific cardboard packaging company's production process.  The chosen company will have detailed data on raw materials sourcing, manufacturing practices, transport methods, and waste management practices. 
3. **MFCA Implementation & Data Collection:** The research team will implement MFCA for the selected company. This involves: 
    * Identifying the key stages of the packaging production process (from raw material extraction to final product delivery). 
    * Mapping out all material flows within each stage and estimating associated carbon emissions from various sources (e.g., energy consumption, transportation, etc.).  
    * Utilizing readily available databases or collecting specific data through interviews with relevant personnel at the company.
4. **Data Analysis & Interpretation:** Data collected will be analyzed using statistical tools and methods to understand key trends in carbon emissions across different stages of production. We will then compare findings to best practices and industry benchmarks to identify areas for improvement. 

**Expected Outcomes:**  This research aims to demonstrate the effectiveness of MFCA as a tool to pinpoint specific carbon emission sources within the cardboard packaging industry. The study's findings can: 
* Provide detailed insights into how different stages contribute to total emissions. 
* Identify high-impact areas for reducing environmental footprint and optimizing sustainability strategies.
* Offer recommendations on implementing effective mitigation measures (like waste reduction, sustainable sourcing, etc.).


**Timeline:**

* **Week 1:** Conduct initial literature review, identify key topics in the field.
* **Week 2-3:** Gather relevant data from case study company, conduct initial interviews and analysis of data.  
* **Week 4-5:** Develop framework for MFCA implementation, collect primary data related to production processes.
* **Week 6-8:** Analyze collected data using statistical tools and methods. Prepare a preliminary report summarizing key findings.
* **Week 9-10:** Draft final report incorporating insights from literature review, case study analysis, and data analysis.
